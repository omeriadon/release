<Callout type="none">
But the people out here had a gentle way as they moved. Arthritic hate. A bent neck that eased an arch at stranger.
</Callout>

The use of sentence length and exclusive language to construct the idea of racial discrimination, highlighting the hatred and views of dominancy held by those who have not yet adapted to the new times.
Furthermore, this reflects upon a modern day society, how the lack of respect is still deemed to be absent in many towns who still share xenophobic views towards First Nations peoples.

<br/>

<Callout className="text-white" type="none">
_White-folk_ kept to their pub on the left ... the _black-folk_ kept to their places across the way.
</Callout>

Anaphora constructs the idea of separation between "black" and "white" folk, juxtaposing the modern day views of acceptance, with explicit emphasis on the racial discrimination Indigenous people are still up against to this day.
Additionally, the italicizing of the words "white-folk" and "black-folk" foregrounds the theme of marginalization as classifying them as two different species rather than of being human, targeting the common perspectives of Aboriginal people modern-day people.
