- Samuel Wagan Watson is a mixed European and Aboriginal author born in 1972 (Early stages of Australia's federation) in Brisbane (East Australia; first colonies).  
- Interested in observations of the everyday world and aboriginal culture. 
- Direct, unstructured writing style, occasionally using capital letters. Majorly influenced by his parents and musicians.

<br/>

Samuel Wagan Watson, born in 1972, is of Munanjali, Bundjalung and European heritage. He is a poet and author, writing ghost stories set in the Southern suburbs of Brisbane and inspired by the oral traditions of spiritual lore passed down from his elders.
"Release" follows his traditional story telling methods, being set in South Western Brisbane, and having the implication of ghosts and/or spirits.

More information about Samuel: https://redroompoetry.org/poets/sam-wagan-watson/

**Cherbourg Mission** - a holding site for forcibly displaced aboriginal people.
Established around 1901-1905. 
One of queensland's largest former reservers
Located near Murgon on Wakka Wakka land.
Residents were restricted:
	- Required permits to leave
	- Unable to speak traditional language
	- Separation of children into dormitories
	- Forced to work with little or no pay
	- Limited central "Ration Shed" for basic supplies such as flour, tea, sugar
